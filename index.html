About Omar

My name is Omar Mostafa, and I’m an Embedded Systems and Battery Engineer. I graduated with a B.S. in Computer Engineering from Purdue University’s Class of 2025.

During my time at Purdue, I gained valuable experience working with Purdue IT in the College of Engineering. There, I helped manage over 15,000 computers and handled software licensing and applications for both students and professors. I developed a strong foundation in network engineering — learning about subnets, IP assignment, and organizational unit (OU) management. This role helped sharpen my skills in computer networks and IoT.

My biggest growth came through Purdue IEEE Racing. I learned the ins and outs of electric vehicle (EV) systems and helped design a high-power battery that earned 1st place in efficiency at EV Grand Prix. I also led the development of a fully autonomous go-kart, integrating a suite of sensors and actuators with an onboard computer running ROS, and an STM32 microcontroller managing outputs via UART.

I was part of the original 4-person team and helped grow it into a 20+ member group full of fresh ideas and energy. As a team, we built the battery system from the ground up — selecting specific cells, designing casings and cooling systems, and welding and mounting everything to the vehicle.

I’m excited to take the skills I’ve developed and help drive innovation in the future of EVs and emerging technologies.

Projects

EV Grand Prix

Designed and built a 20s14p modular battery pack using Molicel P42A 21700 cells, delivering 4.2 kWh capacity and 52.9 kW peak power for a 15kW brushless motor. The pack featured custom-designed water-cooling channels machined into aluminum heat spreaders to support future thermal demands. Copper and nickel hybrid interconnects ensured high conductivity and durable welds, while the modular architecture enabled easy maintenance and scalability. Thermal and electrical tests validated the pack’s performance under peak loads, contributing to a 1st place finish in efficiency at EV Grand Prix.

Autonomous Electric Go-Kart

Engineered a fully autonomous electric go-kart equipped with a 360° LiDAR and stereo camera for environment sensing. Implemented SLAM for real-time localization and mapping, and integrated YOLO for object detection and classification. Sensor data was processed onboard using an NVIDIA Jetson running ROS, with telemetry streamed to a remote base station via a long-range access point. The Jetson communicated control commands to an STM32 microcontroller managing a high-power motor (throttle), stepper motor (steering), and linear actuator (braking). The system achieved full-stack autonomy from perception to actuation.

Analog Neuromorphic Control System for Autonomous EV

Designed and built a spiking neural network (SNN) analog circuit to control an autonomous electric vehicle for lane-following tasks. The system replaces a digital FPGA-based model with an ultra-low-power analog version using custom-designed excitatory and inhibitory synapse circuits. Integrated sensor input was processed in real time to produce motor control outputs, modeled biologically using spike-timing logic. The design was prototyped on a breadboard and is being transitioned to a custom organic MOSFET-based PCB for scalable deployment. Extensive simulation, testing, and validation confirmed performance comparable to digital systems with significantly reduced energy consumption — demonstrating the feasibility of neuromorphic analog hardware in real-time autonomous control.
